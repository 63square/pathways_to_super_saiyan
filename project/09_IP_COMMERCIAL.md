# 09 — IP & Commercial Strategy

## 9.1 Strategic Premise

**Every phase must produce commercially valuable IP and operating businesses sufficient to justify its cost on its own merits, independent of whether the Principal achieves a posthuman phenotype.**

This is the load-bearing assumption of the program's risk model. If we cannot defend the commercial standalone value of each phase, the program reduces to a vanity bet, and the recommended action is to fund only Phase 0 and stop.

## 9.2 Spinout Portfolio Strategy

### 9.2.1 Portfolio Construction Principle

We do not try to build one company that does everything PROMETHEUS does. We build **8–12 focused companies**, each addressing a single therapeutic or commercial vertical, each independently fundable, each acquiring its own outside capital.

This achieves three things:
- **Capital efficiency:** Outside investors fund growth; Holdco capital concentrates on the integration work
- **Risk diversification:** A single spinout failure doesn't damage the portfolio
- **Exit optionality:** Each spinout can be sold or IPO'd on its own timeline

## 9.3 IP Capture & Filing Process

### 9.3.1 Weekly Invention Review

Every Friday afternoon: CSO + GC + outside IP counsel (on call) review the week's work for patentable subject matter.

Criteria:
- Novel: not in public literature
- Non-obvious: more than a routine extension
- Useful: identifiable commercial application
- Disclosable: not a trade secret better protected by secrecy

### 9.3.2 Filing Cadence

```
Day 0:    Invention identified
Day 7:    Invention disclosure document signed by inventor(s)
Day 30:   Provisional patent filed (US)
Day 365:  PCT filed (deferred decision: full patent, abandon, or convert to trade secret)
Day 365+30 months: National phase in priority jurisdictions
```

### 9.3.3 Jurisdictions

Priority filing (always): US, EU, UK, China, Japan
Secondary (case-by-case): Korea, Canada, Australia, India, Brazil
Excluded (cost-benefit fails): minor markets

### 9.3.4 Defensive vs. Commercial Filings

- **Commercial:** intended to be licensed or to support a spinout. ~60% of filings.
- **Defensive:** filed to prevent competitors from blocking program directions. ~30% of filings.
- **Strategic:** filed in jurisdictions where competitor has manufacturing or sales, to create leverage. ~10%.

### 9.3.5 Trade Secret Pool

Some IP is more valuable protected as trade secret:
- Process know-how that cannot be reverse-engineered from product
- Combinations that depend on unpublished SOPs
- Computational pipelines

Trade secret protection includes:
- Restricted access (need-to-know)
- Documented protection measures
- Employee NDAs with criminal-clause
- Marking and tracking

## 9.4 Licensing Strategy

### 9.4.1 Out-Licensing from Holdco to Spinouts

Each spinout receives:
- **Exclusive license** within its defined field
- **Field-limited:** carefully scoped to prevent cannibalization between spinouts
- **Royalty:** 3–8% of net sales (industry standard)
- **Milestone payments:** at IND, Phase 2 readout, approval, $100M sales
- **Reservation:** Holdco retains right to use IP for Principal's own care + research

### 9.4.2 In-Licensing to Holdco

We license selectively from external parties when:
- The technology accelerates the program by >12 months
- The cost is <$10M upfront or <8% royalty
- The licensor is willing to take spinout equity in lieu of cash

Key candidate in-licenses:
- AAV serotype IP from Solid Biosciences, Roche-Spark, or similar
- Reprogramming factor optimization from Altos / Salk
- BCI software stacks from research labs

## 9.5 Exit Strategy by Spinout

### 9.5.1 Default: Acquire-and-Retain Optionality

For each spinout, we plan for two exit paths:
- **Trade sale** to pharma / strategic acquirer at Phase 2 or Phase 3 data
- **IPO** if comparable companies are trading at attractive multiples

The Holdco's share, post-dilution to typical 50–60% by Series B/C, generates substantial cash on exit.

### 9.5.2 Holdco Exit (Long-Term)

The Holdco itself never exits. It is the permanent vehicle for the Principal's program. The Holdco aggregates returns from spinout exits and reinvests in the program.

### 9.5.3 Principal Liquidity

The Principal may, at their option, take partial distributions from the Holdco at any phase gate. Recommended distribution schedule:
- 0% Phase 0
- 10% of spinout exit proceeds Phase 1
- 25% Phase 2
- 50% Phase 3+ if cash-flow positive

This keeps capital deployed where it's needed without trapping the Principal's wealth indefinitely.

## 9.6 Commercial Risk Assessment

### What kills the commercial thesis?

1. **A pharma giant acquires our space pre-emptively** — e.g., Pfizer buys Altos. Probability: 30%. Impact: AeonBio valuation drops 50–70%. Mitigation: portfolio diversification.
2. **A specific intervention fails clinical trials industry-wide** — e.g., partial reprogramming shows oncogenic signal in Phase 2 trials. Probability per intervention: 15–25%. Impact on specific spinout: 60–90% valuation cut. Mitigation: portfolio approach; spinouts fail independently.
3. **Capital markets close to biotech IPOs** — currently a real risk. Probability of multi-year closed window: 40%. Impact: trade-sale exits only, lower multiples. Mitigation: hold privately longer; don't force exits.
4. **Regulatory crackdown on longevity claims** — FDA recently warned several NAD+ producers. Probability of broader action: 30%. Impact: MitoFuel-style consumer spinouts most affected. Mitigation: clinical positioning, evidence-base advertising.

### What boosts commercial thesis?

1. **Altos / Retro / NewLimit succeeds first** — validates the reprogramming space, lifts our AeonBio. Probability of first Phase 2 readout by 2030: 60%.
2. **GLP-1 success continues** — establishes consumer willingness to pay for metabolic interventions, boosts MitoFuel. Already happening.
3. **AGI arrives** — compresses drug discovery timelines; we benefit from in-house AI integration. Probability AGI by 2032: ~30%.
4. **Public longevity sentiment shifts** — gerontology becomes mainstream venture target.

## 9.7 The Anti-Vanity Calculus

A serious test: **if the Principal's transformation fails (zero KPI improvements over baseline), what is the program worth?**

| MitoFuel | $400M | 45% | $180M |
| AeonBio | $2B | 30% | $600M |
| MyoBio | $1B | 35% | $350M |
| HeartoMito | $600M | 40% | $240M |
| NeuroAge | $400M | 40% | $160M |

| Phase 2 spinouts | $3B aggregate | 30% avg | $900M |
| **Failure scenario total** | | | **$2.78B** |

Against ~$862M deployed through Phase 2 in failure scenario, the IRR is approximately **24% annualized** purely from spinout proceeds, even if the integration thesis fails completely.

**This is the commercial argument. The program funds itself through spinouts; the Principal's transformation is upside.**

## 9.8 Conflict of Interest Management

The Principal sits atop a structure that owns equity in companies developing therapeutics the Principal personally uses. This creates several conflict surfaces:

1. **Principal-as-subject + spinout-equity-holder:** ensures no upside in falsifying data on Principal's response (which would tank a spinout's value). Mitigation: external SAB, independent labs, audit trail.
2. **PIs holding spinout equity have incentive to push their own indication:** mitigation: SAB allocates capital, not the PIs.
3. **CMO's care duty vs. CMO equity holdings:** mitigation: CMO equity is in Holdco, not specific spinouts; clear care-above-commercial in CMO contract.

GC reviews all conflict surfaces quarterly. Material conflicts trigger Board review.

## 9.9 Brand & Reputation

The Holdco operates under a neutral name (suggested: **Aurum Biosciences Group** or similar). No reference to PROMETHEUS or to canonical pop-culture power-up phenotypes in any commercial material.

Spinouts each have their own brand identity. Connection to the Principal is not publicized; the Principal does not appear in spinout marketing.

When a spinout exits, the Principal is identified as a private investor only if disclosure is required; never as the subject.
