# 09 — IP & Commercial Strategy (v1.1, Post-SAB Review)

## 9.1 Strategic Premise

**Every phase must produce commercially valuable IP and operating businesses sufficient to justify its cost on its own merits, independent of whether the Principal achieves the operational Super Saiyan phenotype (DEFINITIONS §2.2).**

This is the load-bearing assumption of the program's risk model. If we cannot defend the commercial standalone value of each phase, the program reduces to a vanity bet, and the recommended action is to fund only Phase 0 and stop.

**v1.1 honest recalibration:** v1.0 spinout exit projections were 2021-vintage. Recent comps (Sana 90% peak-to-trough; Unity Biotechnology senolytic Phase 2 failure; Resilience and Generate down-rounds; closed biotech IPO window) require **50% haircut on valuations and 30–40% probability-of-success per spinout**. Updated Monte Carlo in doc 04 §4.7. **Realistic P50 Holdco proceeds: $2.7B over 15 years**, not $8.25B as v1.0 claimed.

## 9.3 IP Capture & Filing Process

### 9.3.1 IP Team (Strengthened per SAB)

v1.0 was under-staffed by ~3× for the disclosure volume implied. Revised:

- **Chief IP Officer** (Phase 1 hire M18) + 3 patent attorneys + 2 paralegals = ~$2.5M/yr fully loaded
- Plus outside counsel (Wilson Sonsini) on retainer
- 1 patent attorney per 25–40 active matters (industry benchmark)

### 9.3.2 Weekly Invention Review

Every Friday: CSO + GC + Head of QA + outside IP counsel review prior week's work.

Criteria: Novel, Non-obvious, Useful, Disclosable.

### 9.3.3 Filing Cadence

```
Day 0:    Invention identified
Day 7:    Invention disclosure document signed by inventor(s)
Day 30:   Provisional patent filed (US) — fast-track 7-day option for high-disclosure-risk
Day 365:  PCT filed (deferred decision: full patent, abandon, or convert to trade secret)
Day 365+30 months: National phase
```

### 9.3.4 Jurisdictions

Priority always: US, EU, UK, China, Japan, Korea, Canada, Australia.

**Filing cost reality (per SAB):** ~$15–25M/yr by Year 3 for 100 disclosures/year at 60% conversion. Now explicit in doc 04 budget (Phase 1+ line items).

### 9.3.5 Defensive Publishing (NEW per SAB)

- **$500K/yr defensive publication budget** (IP.com, Research Disclosure) for ~2000 disclosures
- **Publication-review SOP** with mandatory 60-day IP review before any collaborator submission
- **Liquidated-damages clauses** in collaborator agreements for premature disclosure
- **Conference-monitoring service** ($50K/yr) to catch competitor disclosures starting the §102 clock

### 9.3.6 Trade Secret Discipline (NEW per SAB)

DTSA/UTSA-qualifying:
- **Written trade-secret register** with classification + named custodians, quarterly-audited
- **Air-gapped infrastructure** for crown-jewel pipelines (no cloud, no personal devices)
- **DTSA-compliant marking** on all documents
- **Forensic exit interviews + device imaging** for any departure touching TS material
- **Decide each trade-secret-vs-patent question with 20-year-NPV analysis**: if reverse-engineerable within 10 years, patent it
- ~$1.5M/yr ongoing budget

### 9.3.7 FTO (NEW per SAB)

**$150–300K FTO analysis per spinout pre-formation (~$2–3M total).**
**$20–50M in-licensing war chest** across program.

Particular FTO concerns:
- Senex faces dense Mayo/Unity IP
- MyoBio faces Regeneron/Bristol/Acceleron ActRII patents
- AeonBio faces Altos/Yamanaka thicket

A single dispositive FTO blocker kills a spinout's Series A — FTO is not optional.

## 9.4 Licensing Strategy

### 9.4.1 Out-Licensing to Spinouts

Each spinout: field-limited exclusive license; royalty 3–8% net sales; milestone payments at IND, Phase 2 readout, approval, $100M sales; reservation for Holdco (Principal care + research).

### 9.4.2 In-Licensing to Holdco

Selective. Criteria: accelerates program >12 months, cost <$10M upfront or <8% royalty, licensor willing to take spinout equity in lieu of cash.

Key candidates: AAV serotypes (Solid Biosciences, Roche-Spark), reprogramming optimization (Altos/Salk), BCI software stacks.

## 9.5 Exit Strategy by Spinout

### 9.5.1 Realistic Cap-Table Math (Corrected per SAB)

**v1.0 claimed Holdco 60–80% retention post-Series-A. SAB feedback: 2024–2025 Series A's demand 25–35%/round; full waterfall takes Holdco to 18–28% at exit.**

Updated waterfall used in doc 04 Monte Carlo:
- Seed: Holdco starts 100%, sells 20% → 80%
- Series A: sells 25% → 60%
- Series B: sells 20% → 48%
- Series C: sells 15% → 41%
- IPO + ESOP cumulative ~18% over life → **terminal Holdco ~25–35% at exit if disciplined; 18–25% at major dilutive rounds**

**Option: capital-intensive defense strategy.** Holdco co-invests pro-rata via $200–400M follow-on reserve to defend 50%+ ownership. Costs more, retains more.

### 9.5.2 Pre-Emptive Big Pharma Option Deals (NEW per SAB)

Current strategy assumed trade-sale at Phase 2 — **worst-leverage moment** (single buyer, data-dependent).

Better: **option-to-acquire deals at IND** ($20–50M upfront + $200–500M option exercise) with 2–3 pharmas competing.

- Senex → Lilly or Novartis option (longevity interest)
- AeonBio → Roche or GSK option

Requires **Chief BD Officer hired Month 12** (not Phase 1) with mandate to close 2 option deals by end of Phase 0. ~$1.5M/yr fully loaded.

### 9.5.3 Holdco Exit (Long-Term)

Holdco itself never exits — permanent vehicle for Principal's program. Aggregates spinout returns; reinvests.

### 9.5.4 Principal Liquidity

Distributions:
- 0% Phase 0
- 10% of spinout exit proceeds Phase 1
- 25% Phase 2
- 50% Phase 3+ if cash-flow positive

## 9.9 Conflict of Interest Management (Strengthened per SAB)

### 9.9.1 Bright-Line Rules

- **No individual** involved in the clinical decision to use a compound on the Principal may hold equity in the spinout commercializing that compound
- **CMO, Principal's treating physicians, Science & Safety Committee, EEP** are **categorically excluded** from spinout equity in companies whose products they approved for Principal use
- **PIs and CSO may hold spinout equity** but **must recuse** from the Principal-application decision for their own compound — decision goes to S&S Committee with PI excluded
- **Mandatory written recusal log**, reviewed quarterly by Compensation & Conflicts Committee
- **Founder vesting clawback:** failure to disclose forfeits unvested + 50% vested equity

### 9.9.2 Data-Sharing Ethics (NEW per SAB)

Principal's de-identified data for spinout commercial purposes presents undue inducement (subject = sponsor). Mitigations:

- **EEC binding pre-approval** for each data-transfer-to-spinout instance, with written finding that underlying intervention was clinically (not commercially) motivated
- **Chinese wall** between clinical decisionmaking and spinout valuation — treating physicians no equity, no spinout milestone info, no investor contact
- **Public pre-registration** of Principal's protocols and outcomes on credible registry (ICMJE-compliant) with embargo ≤24 months
- **Explicit prohibition** on intervention whose primary justification is data generation for spinout (EEC determines, not CMO)

GC reviews all conflict surfaces quarterly. Material conflicts trigger Board review.
