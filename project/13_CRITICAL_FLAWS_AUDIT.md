# 13 — Critical Flaws Audit (v1.1 — Resolutions Tracked)

## Purpose

A project plan that cannot self-criticize is a marketing document, not a project plan.

This audit identifies structural weaknesses, what would have to be true for them to bite, the residual mitigation gap, **and (v1.1) the resolution status of each flaw after the SAB-style external review**.

This document is **not** a sales document. It is the honest accounting that the Principal must read before committing capital.

## 13.1 The Strongest Critiques (v1.0) — Resolution Status (v1.1)

### F1 — The Integration Thesis Is Unproven

**The flaw:** Central premise — that multiplicative stacking of 12–16 interventions produces ~50–100× over baseline — has **never been empirically tested**. v1.0 claimed 12–80× envelope.

**v1.1 RESOLUTION:** Multiplicative thesis honestly recalibrated to **3–5× envelope on best axes (biology only)** in doc 02 §2.3. v1.0's product math identified as biophysically naive (double-counting on same physiological axis; antagonisms ignored; AAV-myostatin actually *reduces* capillary-to-fiber ratio so doesn't multiply with VO₂max). Replaced with physiologically-grounded ceilings per metric. **Residual:** still untested in combination in n=1, but now honestly bounded.

### F2 — The n=1 Statistical Problem

**The flaw:** All KPI thresholds compare Principal pre vs post. Within-subject variance comparable to plausible effect sizes for several metrics.

**v1.1 RESOLUTION:** KPI thresholds set generally above typical 18-month within-subject variance. **Head of Biostat** (new role, doc 06 §6.3.8) owns SAP independent of CSO bias. **Public pre-registration on OSF or equivalent** at M0 prevents post-hoc adjustment. **Daily wearable data** establishes baseline noise distribution. Statistical analysis plan locked. **Residual:** cannot bootstrap placebo arm; spinouts must run conventional RCTs to confirm population-level effects (acknowledged commercial path).

### F3 — Próspera Jurisdictional Risk Is Underweighted

**The flaw:** v1.0 scored Próspera collapse P×S=10. SAB review: should be 15.

**v1.1 RESOLUTION:** **Próspera now one of three co-primary jurisdictions** (with UAE/DHCC and El Salvador), not single-jurisdiction bet. All three have standing operating subsidiaries from Day 1. Re-scored to P×S=15 in doc 05. **Residual:** still moderate exposure; mitigated by triad architecture.

### F4 — The CMO Veto Is a Single Point of Failure

**The flaw:** v1.0 had CMO veto absolute, but CMO is paid by Principal and at-will employee.

**v1.1 RESOLUTION:** Multi-part fix:
- **Science & Safety Committee** of Board has binding pre-clearance on first-in-Principal interventions, Tier 2+ initiations, new intervention classes (doc 01 §1.4) — applies from **Phase 0, not deferred to Phase 1**
- **EEC binding veto** on enumerated list (DEFINITIONS §4)
- **CMO 3-year non-terminable contract** with escrowed prepaid comp + personal indemnity insurance + whistleblower carve-out (doc 06 §6.3.2)
- **License in functioning medical board jurisdiction** (not solely Próspera)
- **Two-physician refusal rule** anti-doctor-shopping (doc 11 §11.5)
- **Shadow CMO retained Day 7**, contracted alternate ($50K/yr stipend)

**Residual:** No single individual remains the sole gate. Multiple structural counterweights now in place.

### F5 — Principal Drift Risk

**The flaw:** Principal funds, is subject, is final authority. No legal mechanism compels Principal to honor gate outcomes.

**v1.1 RESOLUTION:** Honest acknowledgment in Charter §1.4 that this is sole-decider program with advisory + reputational + financial-friction counterweights. **Real friction installed:**
- **Ulysses Contract** (doc 12 §12.11): 3-of-11 KPI auto-reduction; 2 Grade 3+ AE 180-day pause; cognitive capacity sunset; age sunset
- **$50M Wind-Down Reserve forfeiture** to institutional entity (not Principal's estate) on any gate override (doc 12 §12.11.2)
- **90-day cooling-off** + written acknowledgment + EEP second-opinion before any override
- **Public pre-registration** of KPI thresholds on OSF — makes drift reputationally visible
- **EEC paid from irrevocable escrow** — cannot be defunded by Principal
- **EEP gate reports delivered simultaneously** to Principal + LID + SAB Chair + Cook Islands trustee — Principal cannot see before LID

**Residual:** Principal retains legal authority. No external constraint is enforceable in court. **This is honestly acknowledged in Charter §1.4** rather than pretended away. Reputational + financial cost is now substantial enough to function as practical constraint.

### F6 — Talent Retention Past Phase 1

**The flaw:** Próspera is not Boston.

**v1.1 RESOLUTION:** Compensation re-benchmarked to 2026 market (doc 04 §4.2.1) — CMO $1.8M base + 100% bonus, etc. Equity packages more generous. Co-primary UAE/DHCC offers more amenities for global talent than Próspera alone. Remote-first for non-wet-lab roles. **Residual:** real risk; may require 25–40% upward comp revision over 10 years (now budgeted in sensitivity analysis doc 04 §4.8).

### F7 — Spinout Recovery Math Assumes Functional Capital Markets

**The flaw:** v1.0 IRR projections used 2020–2024 valuations.

**v1.1 RESOLUTION:** **Monte Carlo re-modeled** (doc 04 §4.7, doc 09): P50 Holdco proceeds = **$2.7B vs v1.0's $8.25B**. 50% haircut on valuations + 30–40% per-spinout success probability + correlated downside applied. **Cap table reality** corrected: Holdco ends at 18–28% post-dilution, not 50%+. **4 new service/software/diagnostics spinouts** added (PrometheusAI, N1Trials, Aurum Clinic, PrometheusDx) with 3–7 year exit cycles to fix v1.0 cash-flow gap. **Residual:** P10 case is $0.4B aggregate — Principal must underwrite the P50, not the mean. Year 11–12 cash-flow positive crossover (was Year 10 in v1.0).

### F8 — The Phase 3+ Bioethical Inflection

**The flaw:** Phase 3 introduces implants, materials, possible cybernetics — qualitatively different ethics.

**v1.1 RESOLUTION:** **EEC binding veto** on Phase 3+ implant interventions. **External ad-hoc bioethics panel** required at G6 (doc 12 §12.8). Pre-mortem mandatory at each gate. **"Never List" expanded** beyond germline (doc 11 §11.13) — includes irreversible interventions, non-removable implants, etc. **Plan preserves option** to stop at any phase.

### F9 — AI Risk Cuts Both Ways

**The flaw:** AGI could obsolete the program or terminate civilization.

**v1.1 RESOLUTION:** Acknowledged unchanged. Same risk as every biotech program. Workstreams designed to be replaceable/upgradeable. China BD/regulatory advisor + monthly competitive intelligence brief added. **Residual:** structural; cannot be mitigated within program scope.

### F10 — The "Vegeta Principle" Cuts Both Ways

**The flaw:** Principal pride is both moat and failure-mode generator.

**v1.1 RESOLUTION:** Same as F5. Real financial + procedural friction now installed.

## 13.3 The Five Residual Flaws (Cannot Be Fully Resolved)

These remain after v1.1 and the Principal must accept them honestly:

### R-F1 — Principal Sole Authority

The Principal funds and is the subject. No external constraint is legally enforceable. v1.1 has installed substantial reputational + financial friction (Ulysses + Wind-Down Reserve + EEC binding veto with escrowed comp + public pre-registration). This is **as much constraint as is structurally possible** without forfeiting the program's existence. The Principal acknowledges this in Charter §1.4.

### R-F2 — Integration Thesis Untested

The 3–5× honest envelope per axis (doc 02 §2.3) is a best-bound, not a tested result. Phase 0 is the first test.

### R-F3 — Phase 3+ Bioethics Are Genuinely Hard

Implants, cybernetics, materials — qualitatively different from longevity-and-performance optimization. v1.1 mitigates with EEC binding veto + external bioethics panel + Never List + 90-day cooling-off, but the **lived experience of being part-cybernetic with subdermal exotic materials** is not something the Principal can fully forecast in advance.

### R-F4 — Capital Market Risk

Biotech valuations remain volatile. P10 Monte Carlo outcome is $0.4B Holdco proceeds against $8.5B inflation-adjusted spend.

### R-F5 — Tail Catastrophe Risk

A Grade 4+ AE on the Principal, despite all SOP + NHP cohort + S&S binding pre-clearance + EEC veto, remains possible at low probability. Independent autopsy + failure investigation pre-contracted with academic pathology service. Captive + commercial reinsurance + cat bond covers financial; reputational cost would be catastrophic and likely program-ending.

## 13.4 The Honest Bottom Line (Updated)

This program is:

- **Scientifically defensible** at Phase 0 level (high confidence)
- **Commercially defensible** through Phase 2 (good confidence; P50 net positive)
- **Ethically defensible** through Phase 2 (strong confidence with EEC + S&S Committee + binding pre-clearance)
- **Operationally feasible** through Phase 2 (moderate confidence; talent + Próspera + insider threat being the main risks)
- **Speculative** at Phase 3+ (real but acknowledged with EEC + bioethics panel gates)
- **Profoundly speculative** at Phase 4 (research with engineering pretensions; may be cancelled)

The program is **not**:

- A guaranteed path to operational Super Saiyan (DEFINITIONS §3 honest probabilities apply)
- A guaranteed path to canonical Super Saiyan (impossible — DEFINITIONS §2.1, appendix A1)
- Risk-free for the Principal (no medical intervention is)
- Cheaper than projected (every program runs over; 20% reserve + $10M medical pause sub-reserve installed)
- Capable of overriding the Principal's own poor judgment (no program can; reputational + financial friction is as much as is structurally possible)

## 13.5 The Capital Allocation Argument (Updated)

**Option A: Project PROMETHEUS v1.1**
- $122M T0 commitment ($62M Phase 0 + $50M Wind-Down + $10M Medical Pause)
- ~70% probability of positive Phase 0 outcome (≥4 of 11 KPIs)
- ~10–15% IRR via spinouts in failure scenarios (down from v1.0's 24% post-correction)
- 25% probability of Phase 3 outcomes satisfying ≥3 of 6 SS operational criteria
- 5–10% probability of Phase 4 satisfying ≥4 (Functional Super Saiyan)
- 5–10% probability of serious personal medical AE through full program
- Asymmetric upside in DEFINITIONS §3.3 (low confidence Phase 4)

**Option B: Conventional Wealth Management**
- ~7% expected IRR (60/40)
- Near-zero personal medical risk
- No transformative outcome
- Capped upside

**Option C: Bespoke Longevity Clinic Subscription**
- $0.5–2M/yr ongoing
- ~10–20% biomarker improvement realistic
- No commercialization upside
- Low medical risk
- Modest outcome

Option A's commercial expected value alone (P50 $2.7B spinout proceeds vs. $62M Phase 0) remains **financially rational** even before personal transformation upside.

Option A's downside is bounded by escrow structure (Phase 0 sunk cost $62M; Wind-Down Reserve $50M to institutional entity if Principal overrides Fail gate).

Option A is the risk-on play with bounded downside and asymmetric upside.

Option B is the safe play. Option C is the cheap version of A. The Principal has presumably already decided B is insufficient.
