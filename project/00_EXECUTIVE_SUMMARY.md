## What Has Changed Since Volume I (Why This Now)

Five technical developments since 2020 have moved this from "speculative" to "executable pilot":

1. **AAV-mediated somatic gene therapy is in human trials** for ~30 indications. The delivery platform exists.
2. **Yamanaka partial reprogramming** (Ocampo 2016, Browder 2022, Yang 2023) has been demonstrated to safely reverse epigenetic age markers in mammals without teratoma formation.
3. **Senolytic + plasma exchange** combinations are in Phase 2 trials at multiple academic medical centers.
4. **High-bandwidth BCIs** are in commercial humans (Neuralink, Synchron — 2024).
5. **Mitochondrial transplantation** has FDA breakthrough designation (McCully protocol, pediatric cardiac).

None of these are speculative. All five are independently fundable as standalone biotech companies *today*. Project PROMETHEUS is the **integration** thesis: that combining 5–8 of these established platforms in a single coordinated subject yields multiplicative gains that no individual program achieves.

## Why the Pilot Will Likely Succeed

Phase 0 deliberately stays inside the envelope of **published peer-reviewed interventions that have already been demonstrated safe in humans, primates, or relevant mammalian models.** No germline editing. No untested novel compounds. No surgical implants other than a standard central line for plasma exchange.

The pilot is designed so that even **partial success** (4 of 8 KPIs) delivers commercially valuable assets:
- Senolytic + plasma exchange protocol — licensable to longevity clinics
- Myostatin/ActRII modulation regimen — licensable to sarcopenia indications
- Microbiome + mitochondrial substrate optimization — licensable as performance protocol
- Multi-omic baselining pipeline — sellable as a standalone CRO offering

**Estimated probability of at least 4 of 8 KPIs hit:** 75%+ (based on independent literature for each component).
**Estimated probability of all 8 hit:** 25–35%.
**Estimated probability of clinically significant adverse event in the Principal:** <5% with the SOP in doc 11.

## What Could Kill the Program

The honest list, in descending order of likelihood:

1. **Principal loses interest or has a competing priority.** Mitigation: time-bounded gates and modest early time-commitment.
2. **A serious adverse event in the Principal during Phase 0.** Mitigation: strict SOP (doc 11), shadow-cohort dogs/primates run on identical protocol 6 months ahead.
3. **Regulatory crackdown in primary jurisdiction (Próspera).** Mitigation: secondary jurisdiction in Cayman or international waters research vessel pre-arranged.
4. **A competing program (Altos, Retro, NewLimit) demonstrates the longevity component first.** Outcome: that's a *win* — we license their result and accelerate.
5. **A founding-team scandal or departure.** Mitigation: founder vesting, redundant deputies, 360 reviews quarterly.
6. **Capital market deterioration affecting spinout exits.** Mitigation: program is self-funded; spinouts are upside, not load-bearing.

Risks 7–30 are quantified in doc 05.
