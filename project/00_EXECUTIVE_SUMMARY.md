## What This Program Is Not (Read DEFINITIONS.md)

This program is **not** trying to produce a literal Dragon Ball Z Super Saiyan. That phenotype violates physical law (energy conservation, Stefan-Boltzmann, Lorentz factor). See appendix A1 and DEFINITIONS §2.1.

This program **is** trying to produce a human satisfying a precisely-defined operational set of criteria (DEFINITIONS §2.2). The realistic Phase 2 outcome is a 3–5× elite human on multiple axes. The aspirational Phase 4 outcome is a "Functional Super Saiyan" (≥4 of 6 operational criteria).

The honest probability assessment (DEFINITIONS §3 and doc 13):

- **~75%** probability of achieving Phase 2 ("Optimized Human" → "Engineered Superhuman" range)
- **~40–60%** probability of Phase 3 satisfying 3+ Super Saiyan operational criteria
- **~10–25%** probability of Phase 4 satisfying 5–6 of 6 criteria, conditional on prior phases succeeding

## What Has Changed Since Volume I (Why This Now)

Five technical developments since 2020 have moved this from "speculative" to "executable pilot":

1. **AAV-mediated somatic gene therapy is in human trials** for ~30 indications. The delivery platform exists.
2. **Yamanaka partial reprogramming** (Ocampo 2016, Browder 2022, Yang 2023) has been demonstrated to safely reverse epigenetic age markers in mammals. **mRNA-LNP reprogramming** (Cao 2024) is now the preferred Phase 1 modality — no integration, dose-tunable, stoppable.
3. **Senolytic + plasma exchange** combinations are in Phase 2 trials at multiple academic medical centers.
4. **High-bandwidth BCIs** are in commercial humans (Neuralink, Synchron — 2024+).
5. **Mitochondrial transplantation** has FDA breakthrough designation (McCully protocol, pediatric cardiac).

None of these are speculative. All five are independently fundable as standalone biotech companies *today*. Project PROMETHEUS is the **integration** thesis: that combining these established platforms in a single coordinated subject yields gains that no individual program achieves.

**Honest revision (post-SAB review):** the integration thesis is **untested in n=1 humans**. Realistic upper bound on combined gain is 3–5× elite baseline per axis, not the 12–80× figure in earlier drafts (now corrected in doc 02 §2.3).

## Why the Pilot Will Likely Succeed (and Why It Might Not)

Phase 0 deliberately stays inside the envelope of **published peer-reviewed interventions demonstrated safe in humans, NHPs, or relevant mammalian models.** No germline editing. No untested novel compounds. No surgical implants other than a standard central line for plasma exchange.

The pilot is designed so that even **partial success** (4 of 11 KPIs) delivers commercially valuable assets, and pure-failure spinout recovery alone (~$50–200M) covers Phase 0 sunk cost.

| Outcome | Probability | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **≥6 of 11 KPIs hit** | 60–75% | Triggers full Phase 1 |
| **4–5 of 11 KPIs hit** | 15–25% | Phase 0 extension (6–12mo) before Phase 1 |
| **≤3 of 11 KPIs hit** | 10–20% | Wind-down or spinout-only continuation |
| **Grade 3+ adverse event in Principal** | <5% | With SOP discipline (doc 11) |
| **Catastrophic (Grade 4+) event** | <1% | With NHP cohort + SOPs + stop rules |

**The probability estimates are intentionally lower than first-draft figures.** This reflects honest re-calibration after independent SAB-style review.

## What Could Kill the Program

The honest list, in descending order of likelihood:

1. **Principal loses interest or has a competing priority.** Mitigation: Ulysses contract; 3-year non-terminable CMO contract; pre-funded escrow that cannot be clawed back without 12-month notice.
2. **Serious adverse event in the Principal during Phase 0.** Mitigation: strict SOP (doc 11); shadow NHP + canine cohort run on identical protocol 6 months ahead; CMO veto; binding Science & Safety Committee pre-clearance.
3. **Próspera regulatory crackdown.** Mitigation: UAE/DHCC and El Salvador as **co-primary** jurisdictions (not contingency) with standing operating subsidiaries from Day 1.
4. **A competing program (Altos, Retro, NewLimit) demonstrates the longevity component first.** Outcome: that's a *win* — we license and accelerate.
5. **A founding-team scandal or departure.** Mitigation: founder vesting cliffs, redundant deputies, shadow CMO on retainer from Day 1.
6. **Capital market deterioration affecting spinout exits.** Mitigation: program is self-funded; revised spinout math (P50 ≈ $2.5–4B Holdco proceeds, not the $8B in earlier drafts).

Risks 7–40 are quantified in doc 05. Critical flaws openly catalogued in doc 13.
