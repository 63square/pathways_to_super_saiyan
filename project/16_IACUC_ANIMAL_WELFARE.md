# 16 — IACUC & Animal Welfare Framework

**Owner:** PI-Reprogramming (Phase 0) + dedicated Veterinary Director (Phase 1)
**Effective:** Charter signing for cohort planning; first animal enrolled Month 6
**Review cadence:** Quarterly committee meeting; annual external audit

## Purpose

The shadow animal cohorts (canine + NHP) are **Gate G2 pass criteria** and gate AAV and mRNA-LNP translation to the Principal. An animal-welfare incident is a top-3 reputational kill risk (R9) with currently zero documented controls in v1.0.

This document closes that gap with an IACUC-equivalent framework appropriate to Próspera (and UAE for any UAE-housed animals), exceeding U.S. PHS Policy and AAALAC International standards where applicable.

## 16.1 Animal Cohorts in Program Scope

### Phase 0
- **Canine cohort (n=12)** — chronic small-molecule + TPE PK/safety only. Mixed-breed adult dogs, screened for health.
- **NHP cohort (Charles River, BIOQUAL, or Bioneeds India contract)** — minimum **6 cynomolgus macaques** for AAV gene-therapy IND-enabling work. **Contracted externally** to a fully-accredited CRO; PROMETHEUS does not house NHPs directly in Phase 0.

### Phase 1
- Canine cohort scaled to 24 for chronic mAb (bimagrumab/garetosmab) + extended TPE schedule + microbiome work.
- NHP cohort scaled to 12+ for mRNA-LNP OSK, autologous mitochondrial isolation, klotho fragment KL1.

### Phase 2+
- Murine cohort added for Dsup transgene + naked mole rat HMM-HA pilots (per doc 10).
- Heterologous mitochondria sourcing (R10 of doc 02) **explicitly EEC binding-veto enumerated list; likely never approved.**

## 16.2 PROMETHEUS Animal Welfare Committee (PAWC)

PROMETHEUS-internal IACUC-equivalent body:

| Seat | Role | Profile |
|---|---|---|
| Chair | Veterinarian (board-certified lab animal medicine) | DACLAM or equivalent |
| 2 | Practicing veterinarian (non-program) | External |
| 3 | Scientist using animals (PI-Reprogramming Phase 0) | Internal |
| 4 | Scientist NOT using animals | Internal cross-check |
| 5 | Non-affiliated community member | External — represents general community concerns |
| 6 | Bioethicist (links to EEC) | External or EEC affiliate |

**PAWC has binding authority** over all program animal work — vetoes are absolute and cannot be overridden by Principal, CEO, CSO, or Board. Mirrors EEC structure for human work.

Compensation: external members $40K/yr cash + $20K/yr in-kind; prepaid 2 years into escrow.

Quorum: 4 members minimum, including Chair + at least 1 external.

## 16.3 The Three R's (Russell & Burch 1959)

Applied to every protocol:

### Replacement
- Use in vitro, organoid, or in silico models where scientifically valid
- Document justification for any animal work (why are non-animal models inadequate?)

### Reduction
- Minimum animal numbers to achieve statistical power
- Justification per protocol (statistical power calculation reviewed by Head of Biostatistics)
- Sequential designs preferred to factorial where possible

### Refinement
- Procedures designed to minimize pain/distress
- Humane endpoints defined (see §16.7)
- Environmental enrichment exceeding regulatory minimums
- Anesthesia/analgesia per IASP and AVMA standards
- Trained handlers; minimum stress contact

**Every protocol documents 3R analysis** before PAWC review.

## 16.4 Husbandry & Housing

### Canine Cohort (Próspera-housed)

- **Pampered Pets-class housing** — co-housed with screened owner-handlers during non-procedure periods (NIH precedent: NHGRI canine genome program); only kenneled during specific procedure windows
- AAALAC-equivalent vet care
- Environmental enrichment: daily exercise, social interaction, foraging tasks
- Health monitoring daily by trained handler; weekly veterinary check
- Diet: high-quality commercial diet + protocol-specific supplementation
- Temperature, humidity, lighting per AAALAC guidance

### NHP Cohort (Externally Housed at CRO)

- Charles River (or BIOQUAL, Bioneeds India) facilities are AAALAC-accredited
- Social housing (pair or group)
- Environmental enrichment per CRO standard (foraging, structures, novel objects)
- Daily welfare assessment
- Procedure-specific anesthesia and analgesia
- PROMETHEUS personnel observe quarterly on-site visits

### General Standards
- All housing meets or exceeds AAALAC International standards
- Veterinary oversight 24/7
- Emergency veterinary response protocol
- Disease surveillance program

## 16.5 Protocol Approval Workflow

```
1. PI drafts protocol with 3R analysis, statistical power calc, humane endpoints
2. Head of Biostat reviews statistical design
3. Veterinary Director reviews husbandry, anesthesia, analgesia, humane endpoints
4. PAWC review (full committee, quorum required)
5. PAWC approves, rejects, or requests modification
6. If approved: protocol enters animal-work registry; staff trained; animals enrolled
7. Periodic in-life review by Veterinary Director
8. Post-study review by PAWC + CSO
```

**No animal procedure without PAWC approval.** Protocol amendments require PAWC re-review.

## 16.6 Pain & Distress Categories

USDA-equivalent categorization:

- **Category B:** No pain, no distress (e.g., observation studies, dietary monitoring)
- **Category C:** Brief or momentary pain (e.g., venipuncture)
- **Category D:** Pain/distress with adequate anesthesia and analgesia (e.g., surgical biopsy under general anesthesia with post-op pain management)
- **Category E:** Pain/distress without alleviation — **prohibited in PROMETHEUS** without specific PAWC + EEC + CSO concurrence and documented scientific necessity that cannot be achieved with category D

Cumulative category E exposure: **Hard cap of zero in Phase 0.**

## 16.7 Humane Endpoints

Pre-defined for every protocol:
- Body condition score thresholds
- Weight loss thresholds (typically >20%)
- Behavioral indicators (lethargy, aggression, social withdrawal)
- Clinical signs (dyspnea, persistent inappetence, neurological signs)
- Tumor size thresholds (for any oncology-adjacent work)
- Pre-defined survival timepoint per study

Reaching humane endpoint = mandatory euthanasia per AVMA Guidelines on the Euthanasia of Animals.

## 16.8 Necropsy & Post-Mortem Documentation

- **All animal deaths (planned or unplanned)** undergo full necropsy
- Pathology by board-certified veterinary pathologist
- Findings documented; entered into program database (Tier-Amber)
- Unexpected findings trigger PAWC review + potential protocol amendment

For NHP cohort: necropsy at contracted CRO with PROMETHEUS Veterinary Director observation rights.

## 16.10 Activist Litigation Mitigation (R9)

The animal program is a documented activist-litigation target. Mitigations:

- **Compliance with most-restrictive applicable regime** (effectively U.S. PHS Policy + AAALAC) even where local law is less stringent — defeats "regulatory arbitrage" framing
- **Transparency selectively used** — peer-reviewed publications include animal welfare statements; PAWC composition acknowledged publicly
- **Pre-emptive engagement** with major animal welfare organizations (HSUS, World Animal Protection, Animal Welfare Institute) on protocol design
- **No "ag-gag" reliance** — assume public scrutiny is possible
- **Counter-disclosure** prepared (Posture White, doc 17 BCP) on animal welfare practices

Activist litigation is monitored quarterly by GC + CSecO.

## 16.13 End-of-Life and Adoption

For canine cohort:
- Default at study end: **adoption to screened homes** (preferentially the handler-owners they were housed with)
- Health certification before adoption
- 12-month post-adoption welfare check-in
- Lifetime medical fund: $10K per dog escrowed at enrollment

For NHP cohort:
- Per CRO end-of-study protocols
- Where scientifically and welfare-appropriate, sanctuary placement (Chimp Haven analog) explored

**Euthanasia only when humane endpoint reached or scientific necessity (e.g., tissue harvest); never default end-of-study disposition.**
