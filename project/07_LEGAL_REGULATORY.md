### 7.1.1 Why Each Entity (Updated)

**Cook Islands Trust + Nevis LLC interlay:**
- Cook Islands provides strongest debtor protection statute (2-year statute of limitations on fraudulent conveyance, creditors must re-litigate in Cook Islands jurisdiction)
- Nevis LLC charging-order protection inserts second layer
- Principal as **non-current** discretionary beneficiary defeats sham-trust attacks (per *FTC v. Affordable Media*); non-US protector with letter of wishes structure
- Flight clause + named successor trustees in Belize and Nevis defeat single-jurisdiction freeze
- Does NOT insulate against criminal forfeiture (acknowledged)

**Cayman Holdco:** No corporate income tax; English-law commercial framework; sophisticated financial sector; IP holding and spinout equity rollup. **Clinical operations NEVER conducted here.**

**"Liability NewCo":** New v1.1 — bifurcates operational liability from IP-holding entity. Clinical work conducted in jurisdictional subsidiaries (Próspera, UAE, El Salvador), each rolled up to a single Liability NewCo. A clinical liability cannot reach IP value.

**Delaware US LLC:** US employment of US-based staff (Boston satellite). **Restricted scope: analytics, discovery, IP work only. No protocol authorship; no clinical decisionmaking.** Backed by annual opinion-of-counsel from white-collar firm.

**Bermuda Captive + Guernsey Side-Car:** Self-insurance for medical malpractice + D&O + Side A DIC + cyber + K&R. Side-car in second domicile prevents single-regulatory-event impairment.

**Each Spinout (Delaware C-corp):** Standard VC-fundable; **structured for §1202 QSBS eligibility** (potentially $10M+ federal-tax-free gain per spinout per shareholder).

## 7.2 Jurisdictional Strategy — CO-PRIMARY TRIAD

### Primary Triad (NOT single jurisdiction)

| Jurisdiction | Role | Standing |
|---|---|---|
| **Próspera ZEDE, Honduras** | Primary R&D + clinical | Standing operating subsidiary with active GMP relationships |
| **UAE / Dubai Healthcare City (DHCC)** | Co-primary clinical (DHA Therapeutic Innovation pathway) | Standing operating subsidiary from Day 1; clinical-suite leased Mediclinic City Hospital initially |
| **El Salvador** | Secondary clinical + R&D | Standing operating subsidiary from Day 1 (small footprint) |

**All three operate from Day 1, not as contingency.** This is the most important v1.1 fix.

**Próspera-specific risk:** Honduran political environment hostile to ZEDEs. ICSID arbitration *Honduras Próspera Inc. v. Honduras* (Case No. ARB/23/2) pending. ZEDE framework may collapse before Phase 2 (P estimated 20–40%). Co-primary structure eliminates single-point dependency.

### Tertiary Backup

- **Cayman Islands:** Holdco domicile, can host limited clinical if needed
- **St. Kitts:** Caribbean stem cell tourism corridor history
- **BVI:** Alternate Holdco refuge

### Removed (per ethics review)

- **"International waters research vessel"** — removed entirely as intervention site (logistics/recovery only). Ethically indefensible per SAB feedback. A vessel for *crew transport* or *medical recovery* is fine; conducting novel interventions on the Principal in international waters specifically because they're unlawful on land is not.

### Excluded: United States, EU, UK

The program **never** performs interventional procedures on the Principal in US/EU/UK. Research, IP, analytics, employment of US/EU/UK staff in non-interventional roles is fine and necessary.

**Boston satellite explicit scope restriction:** discovery, analytics, IP. **No protocol authorship.** This is documented in Boston staff job descriptions and reinforced annually. Backed by opinion-of-counsel.

## 7.3 Specific Regulatory Considerations

### 7.3.1 Off-Label Use (Revised Framing)

Off-label *prescribing* is legal. Off-label *combination protocols designed at scale, documented in SOPs, applied systematically* edges toward "practice of investigation" requiring an IND under 21 CFR 312.2(b). **All protocol design occurs in Próspera/Cayman, not Boston.** Boston staff job descriptions explicitly exclude protocol authorship.

For the most defensible interventions, consider **pre-emptive single-patient expanded-access IND** which converts legal posture from "evasion" to "compliance."

### 7.3.2 AAV Gene Therapy (Demoted to Phase 1+ Escalation)

Per doc 02 v1.1, **AAV is no longer first-line.** mAb (bimagrumab/garetosmab) first; mRNA-LNP OSK first. AAV only as escalation if mAb/mRNA paths fail.

When AAV is administered: GMP manufacture at Próspera-licensed facility or contracted GMP CMO. Documented to ICH-GCP+ standard. **Medical records segregation:** the Principal's identifiable data never leaves Tier-Red enclave; commercial procurement uses alias billing.

### 7.3.3 Partial Reprogramming — Now mRNA-LNP

mRNA-LNP OSK (Cao 2024) replaces AAV-OSK as first-line. No integration risk, dose-tunable, stoppable. Phase 1 administration gated on (a) external human Phase 1 readout from Altos/Retro/NewLimit OR (b) program's NHP 24-month data.

### 7.3.4 Mitochondrial Transplantation

**Autologous only** for Phase 1. Heterologous (cetacean/avian) explicitly on EEC binding-veto enumerated list and may never be approved.

### 7.3.5 BCI Implantation (Phase 2 earliest)

Commercial device only (Neuralink, Synchron, or successor) with clearance in at least one major jurisdiction. Principal travels to cleared jurisdiction (likely US or UK) for implantation; subsequent care at Próspera/UAE.

### 7.3.6 Germline — ABSOLUTE PROHIBITION

No germline edits at any phase. No embryo work. No exception. Bright-line.

### 7.3.7 Xenotransplantation (Phase 3+)

Per current FDA-cleared protocols at US center if specific organ indication arises. EEC binding-veto pre-approval required.

### 7.3.8 Export Controls + Sanctions (NEW)

**OFAC / EAR / BIS / Australia Group compliance program** required:
- Named OFAC/EAR compliance officer under GC
- SDN / Entity List / UVL screening of every CMO, supplier, SAB member, EEC member
- **EAR Category 1C353** review for AAV vectors, plasmids, GMOs
- **MMPA** (16 USC §1372 extraterritorial) opinion **before any cetacean tissue work** — likely concludes prohibited; backstop for EEC veto
- **Pelly Amendment** review
- **ITAR** check on Phase 4 directed-energy / hardware references
- **CITES** review for any tissue from listed species

### 7.3.9 FCPA / UKBA Compliance (NEW)

ZEDE councilors and Próspera Health regulators likely "foreign officials" under 15 USC §78dd-1. UAE DHA officials similarly. Required:
- Written FCPA/UKBA compliance program with annual training
- DOJ-style risk assessment of all government touchpoints
- Prohibition on facilitation payments (even where locally tolerated)
- Due diligence on every Próspera/UAE/ES-affiliated counterparty
- Books-and-records discipline sufficient to survive FCPA accounting-provisions inquiry
- Quarterly compliance officer report to GC + LID

## 7.4 Informed Consent Framework (Strengthened)

The Principal signs:

1. **Master Consent** (per phase, refreshed each gate)
2. **Procedure-Specific Consent** (per intervention) — 14-day cooling-off first-time, 24-hour repeat
3. **Data Use Consent** (annual) — **explicit prohibition on data-transfer-to-spinout where primary justification is data generation rather than clinical benefit to Principal**; EEC binding approval for each transfer instance
4. **Advance Directive** (quarterly updated, parallel directives executed under each operating jurisdiction's formalities to defeat recognition gaps)
5. **NEW: Capacity Acknowledgment** — annual baseline MacCAT-CR; quarterly during active intervention phases
6. **NEW: Therapeutic Misconception Screen** — quarterly, by external psychiatrist + bioethicist dyad
7. **NEW: Ulysses Pre-commitment** at G0 (see doc 12 §12.11)

All consent witnessed by GC + Principal's **independent counsel** (separate firm from GC). 14-day cooling-off for first-time interventions, 24 hours for repeat.

**Ulysses clause architecture:** parallel directives across operating jurisdictions, recognizing that Ulysses clauses are void in some jurisdictions — re-paper if Principal moves jurisdictions.

**Designated medical proxy + alternate + tertiary** (per doc 06 §6.8).

## 7.5 IP Strategy (Strengthened per SAB)

### 7.5.1 Ownership

All employee inventions during program work owned by Holdco (employment agreements assign). **Per-jurisdiction compliance:**
- California Labor Code §2870 carve-outs respected
- Massachusetts §19 (2018) compliance
- German ArbnErfG (statutory inventor remuneration) — material in EU hires
- UK Patents Act §39-41 (post-*Shanks v. Unilever* exposure)

**SAB IP terms tightened:** SAB members **pre-assign** rights for program-funded work with reasonable royalty (replaces v1.0's "right of first refusal at fair market rate").

### 7.5.2 Filing Strategy

- Provisional patents: filed within 30 days
- 7-day fast-track provisional option for high-risk-of-disclosure inventions
- PCT applications: within 9 months
- National phase: US, EU, UK, China, Japan, Korea, Canada, Australia
- **Defensive publication queue** ($500K/yr budget) for non-core inventions

### 7.5.3 Licensing & Spinouts

Each spinout: field-limited exclusive license, royalty 3–8% net sales, milestone payments, reservation of rights for Principal care + research.

**Spinout Committee** (CEO + CSO + GC + independent director + lead investor observer): supermajority to spin out; default is retain.

### 7.5.4 Trade Secret Discipline (NEW — DTSA/UTSA Qualifying)

- **Written trade-secret register** with classification levels + named custodians, updated quarterly
- **Air-gapped infrastructure** for crown-jewel pipelines (no cloud, no personal devices)
- **DTSA-compliant marking** on all documents
- **Forensic exit interviews + device imaging** for departures touching trade secrets
- Quarterly audit by Compensation & Conflicts Committee
- 20-year-NPV analysis on each: patent vs. trade secret decision

### 7.5.5 FTO (NEW)

**$150–300K FTO analysis per spinout pre-formation.** $20–50M in-licensing war chest budgeted. Senex faces dense Mayo/Unity IP; MyoBio faces Regeneron/Bristol/Acceleron ActRII; AeonBio faces Altos/Yamanaka thicket. FTO is not optional.

## 7.6 Data Governance — Cross-Border Architecture (NEW per SAB)

### 7.6.1 Data Tiers (Revised — Per SAB Architecture)

**Tier-Red (Principal genomic, methylation, identifiable multi-omic):**
- True air-gap. No network interface. WORM optical media only. No wireless radios within 3m. TEMPEST-rated enclave at Próspera HQ.
- **Threshold cryptography:** 3-of-5 hardware-key shares held by named officers in separated jurisdictions (not single-individual access).
- All access logged immutably + quarterly audit.

**Tier-Amber (routine labs, wearables, non-identifiable analytics):**
- Segmented clinical VLAN with one-way data-diode push to Tier-Red.
- HL7/FHIR ingestion through inspected proxy.
- Encrypted at rest.

**Tier-Green (corporate, finance, M&A, communications):**
- Separate AD forest from clinical.
- No trust relationships with Tier-Amber/Tier-Red.
- Standard enterprise controls.

**Four-zone segmentation enforced by physical firewalls with documented ACLs. Quarterly red-team explicitly testing lateral movement between zones.**

### 7.6.2 Cross-Border Compliance (NEW)

Principal data crosses Próspera, UAE, Cayman, Boston jurisdictions. Required architecture:

- **Single legal-basis architecture:** Principal as data subject signs explicit GDPR Art. 9(2)(a) consent naming every recipient jurisdiction
- **Intragroup Data Transfer Agreement** with 2021 SCCs + Transfer Impact Assessment (post-Schrems II)
- **HIPAA BAAs** for Boston-side vendors; Boston entity kept in **research-only posture** under Common Rule (not HIPAA-covered) where possible
- **Pseudonymization-at-ingest** for Boston subset — never holds re-identifiable Principal data
- **Cayman DPA 2017** transfer adequacy review
- **UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 45/2021** compliance for UAE-side processing
- **Honduran Ley de Protección de Datos Personales** compliance

### 7.6.3 Right to Be Forgotten

Principal can demand destruction of any data at any time, with GC opinion on legal/insurance retention. Default: destruction on Principal request after statutes lapse.

## 7.10 Criminal-Exposure Firewall for US-Person Staff (NEW per SAB)

US-person directors of Cayman Holdco + US-employed Boston staff face potential exposure under 18 USC §1956 (money laundering predicated on FD&C Act violations), §371 conspiracy, Travel Act, if program offshore interventions characterized as evasion.

Required:
- **No US persons on the boards** of Próspera operating subsidiary or Liability NewCo (clinical-decisioning entities)
- **Clear functional separation** — Boston entity discovery/analytics/IP only, written scope
- **Annual opinion-of-counsel** from US white-collar firm on structural lawfulness, supports good-faith defense
- **D&O coverage with Side A DIC** for non-indemnifiable claims, separate from med-mal tower
- **Personal indemnification** of US-person staff by Holdco for program-related claims (within program-related scope)
