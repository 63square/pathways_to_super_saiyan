## 1. Why This Document Exists

Many people who will join Project PROMETHEUS — scientists, clinicians, legal counsel, security staff, investors in the spinouts — will not know, or will misunderstand, what the program is actually trying to achieve. The word "Super Saiyan" appears in the source white papers and the internal vernacular, but it is a **codename and aspirational metaphor**, not a literal target.

This document gives the only authoritative definition of:

1. What the program means by **"Super Saiyan"** (Section 2)
2. What the program is **actually committed to delivering** (Section 3)
3. What is explicitly **out of scope** (Section 4)
4. The **measurable success criteria** that determine whether the goal has been met (Section 5)
5. How to **talk about the program** internally and externally (Section 6)

If any other program document conflicts with this one, **this document controls**. Any proposed redefinition must be ratified by the Principal + Board + External Ethics Committee.

## 2. Formal Definition of "Super Saiyan"

### 2.1 The Pop-Culture Source

In Akira Toriyama's *Dragon Ball Z* (1989–present), a "Super Saiyan" is a transformed state of a fictional alien species (Saiyans) that grants:

- Order-of-magnitude or greater increases in physical strength, speed, and durability
- Projected energy attacks ("ki blasts") of arbitrary magnitude
- Levitation and flight
- Visible aura (golden, blue, or red depending on the form)
- Hair color and eye color change (typically golden hair, turquoise eyes)
- Dramatic emotional and physiological trigger conditions

**The canonical Super Saiyan, as depicted, is physically impossible.** Volume I of the founding white paper (`appendices/A1_volume_I_findings.md`) catalogs the specific physical-law violations: conservation of energy, the averaged null energy condition, Stefan-Boltzmann radiation limits, and Lorentz-factor energy costs.

**Project PROMETHEUS is not attempting to produce the canonical Super Saiyan.** Any staff member who joins believing otherwise has been misinformed and should leave.

### 2.2 The Operational Definition Used By This Program

For the operational purposes of Project PROMETHEUS, **"Super Saiyan" is defined as a state of human enhancement satisfying ALL of the following criteria simultaneously:**

| Domain | Criterion (must be true at terminal Phase 3+) |
|---|---|
| **D1 — Physical performance** | Sustained performance ≥10× the median of elite-trained age-matched humans on at least 3 of: VO₂max, peak power output, time-to-exhaustion, peak isokinetic force, impact tolerance. |
| **D2 — Healthspan** | Biological age (measured by composite of DNAm-based clocks) at least 20 years younger than chronological age, with no clinical organ-system disease. |
| **D3 — Recovery** | Recovery from severe musculoskeletal injury (e.g., complete tendon rupture, long-bone fracture) in ≤25% of standard clinical timeline, without loss of function. |
| **D4 — Sensory & cognitive** | At least one objectively measurable sensory or cognitive capability beyond the human normal range (e.g., sub-50ms visuomotor reaction, infrared vision via implant, expanded working memory via BCI). |
| **D5 — Projected force capability** | Ability to deliver, on demand, kinetic or directed-energy output at least 1,000× sustained metabolic output, via integrated wearable or implanted systems. |
| **D6 — Visible signature** | A visually distinguishable physiological state ("transformation") that correlates with elevated performance — engineered via bioelectric, fluorescence, or photonic means. This is the lowest-priority criterion; it exists for completeness with the source canon but is not load-bearing. |

A subject who satisfies **all six** is, by this program's operational definition, a Super Saiyan.

**This definition is the formal one. It is what success looks like. It is not the canonical Toriyama depiction, and it does not pretend to be.**

### 2.3 What the Operational Definition Is Not

The operational definition is deliberately:

- **Measurable.** Every criterion is quantitative or has a binary objective test.
- **Achievable in principle within current scientific frameworks** (per Volume II of the white paper, restricted to currently-feasible or near-feasible technology).
- **Falsifiable.** If at Phase 3 the subject does not meet ≥4 of 6 criteria, the program has failed to achieve its operational goal.
- **Not magical.** No criterion requires a violation of known physical law.
- **Not aesthetic.** Hair color, eye color, and the precise color of the aura are explicitly not gating criteria. They may be pursued as cosmetic adjuncts in Phase 4 but are not what success means.

## 3. The Program's Actual Commitments

In descending order of confidence:

### 3.1 What Project PROMETHEUS commits to producing (high confidence)

By the end of **Phase 2 (~Month 60)**, the Principal will be characterized by:

- Top-decile elite-human performance on at least 3 of the 11 KPIs (doc 08)
- Biological age trajectory inverted relative to chronological age
- A documented, peer-reviewable-quality phenotype superior to baseline elite humans
- A portfolio of 8–12 spinout companies built on the program's intermediate findings

This is the **floor** of program ambition. It is independently valuable, commercially recoverable, and ethically defensible. The Principal should regard this floor as the realistic expected outcome.

### 3.2 What Project PROMETHEUS aims to produce (moderate confidence)

By the end of **Phase 3 (~Year 10)**, the Principal may additionally exhibit:

- 10× elite-human performance on 3+ KPIs
- Subdermal materials reinforcement (graphene/spider-silk composites)
- Integrated BCI with sub-50ms reflex loop and tactical co-processor
- Wearable directed-energy capability (kinetic projection)
- Functional regeneration capacity at the limb level

This is the **target** of program ambition. It satisfies criteria D1–D4 of the Super Saiyan operational definition. Probability of achieving most components: 40–60%.

### 3.3 What Project PROMETHEUS aspires to produce (low confidence)

By the end of **Phase 4 (~Year 15–25)**, if and only if intervening phases succeed, the Principal may additionally exhibit:

- Subdermal nuclear power source
- Subdermal directed-energy emitter (the "ki blast" engineering equivalent)
- Bioelectric morphogenetic field stabilization (Levin-program-derived)
- Engineered visible aura (criterion D6)
- 100× or greater performance on selected KPIs
- 200-year projected healthspan

This is the **ceiling** of program ambition. It satisfies all six criteria of the operational definition. Probability: 10–25%, conditional on Phases 0–3 having succeeded.

## 4. Explicit Out-of-Scope

The following are **not** goals of Project PROMETHEUS. Any staff member who believes the program is pursuing these is misinformed:

| Out of scope | Reason |
|---|---|
| Canonical Dragon Ball Z Super Saiyan (literal) | Violates physical law (Volume I) |
| Germline modification | Criminalized in primary and secondary jurisdictions; bright-line program prohibition |
| Modification of human subjects other than the Principal | Out of scope by Charter |
| Whole-brain emulation / upload | Destroys the Principal; only contemplated in posthumous-option framework with separate consent |
| Antimatter or vacuum-energy power sources | Speculative beyond engineering horizon |
| Flight without propulsive or aerodynamic support | Violates conservation of momentum |
| Time dilation chambers (real Lorentz factor >1.01) | Energy requirements many orders beyond civilization |
| Telekinesis, telepathy, or other psi phenomena | No engineering substrate; not pursued |
| Religious or supernatural framings of "ki" | The program treats "ki" as a metaphor for measurable bioelectric/morphogenetic state, nothing more |
| Immortality | Healthspan extension is in scope; biological immortality is not claimed |
| Any intervention whose primary mechanism is irreversible without explant or rescue pathway | Categorically prohibited per doc 11 Never List |

## 5. Measurable Success Criteria — How We Know If We Succeeded

### 5.1 Operational Success (the "Super Saiyan" definition is met)

**The Principal meets ≥4 of 6 criteria D1–D6 by Phase 3 conclusion.** Specifically:

- D1: ≥10× elite-median on 3+ physical KPIs (objective)
- D2: ≥20-year biological-age delta from chronological (DNAm clocks)
- D3: Documented ≥4× recovery acceleration vs. clinical baseline
- D4: ≥1 sensory or cognitive capability outside normal range
- D5: ≥1000× peak directed-energy projection vs. metabolic baseline
- D6: Visible engineered transformation signature

A subject meeting **all 6** is, by this document's authority, a **Super Saiyan** in the operational sense. A subject meeting **4–5** is a **Functional Super Saiyan**. A subject meeting **2–3** is an **Engineered Superhuman**. A subject meeting **0–1** is an **Optimized Human** — still a substantial achievement, but not the operational goal.

### 5.2 Commercial Success (the financial thesis is met)

- Holdco net proceeds across spinout portfolio: P50 ≥ $2.5B by Year 15
- At least 3 spinouts at $1B+ valuation
- Program cash-flow positive by Year 13

### 5.3 Scientific Success (independent of Principal outcome)

- ≥50 peer-reviewed publications across program portfolio
- ≥100 issued patents
- ≥2 platform technologies adopted by external academic or clinical groups
- Public pre-registration of all Phase 0 protocols completed at Month 0

A program that achieves Scientific and Commercial Success but **fails** Operational Success has still delivered substantial value. This is the asymmetric-upside thesis.

## 6. How to Talk About the Program

### 6.1 Internal Vocabulary

- **"The Principal"** — the subject and funder. Never use the Principal's real name in writing.
- **"Project PROMETHEUS"** — the program codename. Used in internal documents only.
- **"The integration thesis"** — the hypothesis that combining multiple enhancement modalities produces multiplicative gains. The proprietary insight.
- **"Super Saiyan"** — used **only** in informal internal communications, and only with reference to the operational definition in Section 2.2. Never in written external materials. Never with external counsel, regulators, press, or investors.
- **"Functional Super Saiyan"** — acceptable informal shorthand for "Principal meeting ≥4 of 6 criteria."
- **"Engineered posthuman"** — preferred written internal term.

### 6.2 External Vocabulary

- **"Longevity and human-performance research program"** — preferred external description.
- **"Engineered performance enhancement"** — acceptable for technical external audiences.
- **"N=1 self-experimental medical optimization"** — acceptable for medical and regulatory audiences.
- **"Aurum Biosciences Group"** (or whichever neutral brand name is adopted) — the externally-facing entity name.

Any external communication referencing "Super Saiyan," "Dragon Ball," or canonical depictions must be pre-cleared by GC and Crisis Comms. The program's external posture is not ironic; the codename is internal only.

### 6.3 With the Principal

- The Principal may use whatever vocabulary they prefer in private.
- In all formal program meetings, the operational definition (Section 2.2) is the binding referent.
- The CMO is responsible for ensuring the Principal understands the difference between the canonical depiction and the operational goal. This understanding is documented in each Master Consent.

### 6.4 With New Staff (Onboarding)

This document is part of the Day-1 onboarding package for every hire above the level of contract technician. Acknowledgment is recorded.

A common onboarding script for clarification:

> "You will hear the phrase 'Super Saiyan' used internally. It is a codename for a precisely-defined enhancement target documented in DEFINITIONS.md. The program does not believe in, and is not attempting to produce, the literal cartoon character. The actual goal is documented in Section 2.2 and 5.1 of that document. You will be asked, during your first week, to sign acknowledgment that you have read and understood it. If you have ever told anyone, including in a job interview, that the program is trying to produce a literal Dragon Ball Z character, please correct that as soon as possible — that statement is wrong and damages the program's credibility."

## 7. The Honest Question Many Will Ask

> "If the goal isn't a real Super Saiyan, why do you call it that?"

Because the underlying engineering target — a human being who is order-of-magnitude superior to baseline across measurable physical, longevity, recovery, sensory, cognitive, and projected-energy axes — happens to overlap substantially with the cartoon depiction in the dimensions that matter for combat and longevity, while diverging in the dimensions that matter for physics. The codename captures the ambition. The operational definition (Section 2.2) captures the engineering reality.

The Principal funded this program after reading a tongue-in-cheek scientific analysis of Dragon Ball Z. The program's institutional discipline begins with **separating the inspiration from the deliverable.** This document is where that separation is formalized.
