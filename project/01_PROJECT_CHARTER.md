## 1.1 Mission Statement

Project PROMETHEUS exists to deliver, within 25 years and $4–8B, a human individual ("the Principal") who satisfies the **operational Super Saiyan definition** in DEFINITIONS.md §2.2 — that is, who meets ≥4 of 6 measurable criteria across physical performance, healthspan, recovery, sensory/cognitive capability, projected force capability, and visible transformation signature — using only currently-feasible or near-feasible (5-year horizon) biological, medical, and engineering interventions, while building a portfolio of commercially viable spinout companies whose aggregate value exceeds program cost regardless of the Principal's terminal phenotype.

**Realistic floor commitment (Phase 2):** an "Engineered Superhuman" (≥2–3 criteria met). **Aspirational ceiling (Phase 4):** a "Super Saiyan" by operational definition (≥4 of 6 criteria). See DEFINITIONS §3 for honest probability assessment.

## 1.2 Scope

### In Scope
- Somatic (non-germline) genetic and epigenetic interventions in the Principal
- Pharmacological enhancement protocols
- Surgical implants (medical-grade, FDA-cleared or jurisdictional-equivalent class)
- Brain-computer interfaces (commercial or research-grade, with explicit consent)
- Engineered exoskeletal / wearable augmentation
- Nutritional, training, and environmental optimization
- Cell therapy and autologous mitochondrial transplantation
- Therapeutic plasma exchange and senolytic regimens
- Diagnostic, biomarker, and connectomic baselining
- Spinout company formation and commercialization
- All necessary legal, regulatory, financial, and security infrastructure

### Out of Scope (See DEFINITIONS §4 for the complete list)
- **Germline modification of any kind** — explicitly prohibited
- **Modifications to other humans** (no embryos, no employees-as-subjects)
- Any intervention not supported by published peer-reviewed literature or active human clinical trial
- Anything requiring violation of host-country criminal law
- **"International waters research vessel" as intervention site** — removed (ethically indefensible; logistics/recovery only)
- "Speculative physics" interventions — research-monitoring only

### Reserved for Phase 3+ (require separate Board + External Ethics Committee approval)
- Whole-organ xenotransplantation into the Principal
- Subdermal nuclear power sources
- Subdermal directed-energy emitters
- Heterologous mitochondrial donor sourcing (cetacean, avian)
- Cybernetic substitution beyond clinically indicated prostheses

## 1.3 Objectives (SMART)

### Phase 0 (Months 0–18) — Pilot
1. **Baseline** the Principal across 47 pre-registered biomarkers by Month 4
2. **Demonstrate** statistically significant improvement on **6+ of 11 KPI axes** (expanded from 8 per SAB feedback — see doc 08) by Month 16
3. **Achieve** zero Grade 3+ adverse events
4. **Spin out** at least one company with $20M+ Series A commitment by Month 18
5. **Document** all protocols to GMP-equivalent standard under formal QMS (doc 15)

### Phase 1 (Months 18–36) — Foundation Build
6. **Establish** primary R&D facility in Próspera ZEDE + co-primary in UAE/DHCC + secondary in El Salvador
7. **Recruit** core team to 100 FTE
8. **Demonstrate** 3× elite-baseline performance on at least one KPI in the Principal
9. **Generate** $50M+ in spinout equity events

### Phase 2 (Months 36–60) — Integration
10. **Demonstrate** 5× elite-baseline on at least three KPIs (revised downward from 10× per honest re-calibration)
11. **Stand up** the Augmentation Suite (centrifuge, hypobaric chamber, BCI lab)
12. **Build** the IP portfolio to >100 issued patents
13. **Begin** Principal satisfying ≥2 of 6 Super Saiyan operational criteria (DEFINITIONS §2.2)

### Phase 3+ (Years 5–15)
14. Target satisfying ≥4 of 6 operational criteria. Subject to separate Board + EEC reauthorization.

## 1.4 Governance

### Honest Acknowledgment

**This program is structurally a sole-decider program.** The Principal funds it, is the sole subject, and ultimately controls the institutional resources. No legal mechanism — Board vote, SAB veto, External Ethics Committee — can compel the Principal to do or not do something with their own body.

The governance structure that follows is therefore composed of **(a) advisory bodies that can resign publicly**, **(b) procedural friction that buys time**, **(c) public pre-registration that makes drift reputationally visible**, and **(d) pre-pledged financial penalties (the Wind-Down Reserve) that make override costly.**

These are real constraints in the sense that ignoring them costs reputation, talent, and money. They are not constraints in the sense that a court will enforce them against the Principal's contrary instruction.

The Principal acknowledges this design honestly by Charter signature.

### Board Composition (5 seats, codified)

- **Seat 1** — Principal or Principal's designee (Chair only if no LID structure adopted)
- **Seat 2** — Senior biotech operator (former public-co biotech CEO/Chair). **Independent.**
- **Seat 3** — Former regulator or bio-focused jurist (ex-FDA CDER/CBER, ex-EMA, or senior bio-regulatory partner). **Independent.**
- **Seat 4** — Bioethicist with public-company or major-institution board experience (NOT drawn from SAB). **Independent.**
- **Seat 5** — Independent fiduciary representing Cook Islands trust / spinout equity holders. **Independent.**

**Hard rule: 3 of 5 must meet NYSE/Nasdaq-equivalent independence tests.** No material financial relationship with Principal in prior 3 years, no employment, no family ties, no spinout equity at issuance.

**Lead Independent Director (LID):** elected annually by the three independents (Seats 2, 3, 4). LID:
- Chairs executive sessions without the Principal at every Board meeting
- Sets agenda jointly with the Principal
- Is the named recipient for whistleblower escalations
- Has standing to call an unscheduled Board meeting over the Principal's objection

**No Board member may hold spinout equity at issuance**; post-departure cooling-off of 24 months before joining a spinout board or accepting spinout equity.

### Board Committees

- **Audit & Finance Committee** (3 independents): External audit, captive insurance, financial flows
- **Science & Safety Committee** (LID + bioethicist + 2 rotating SAB members): **BINDING pre-clearance authority** over (a) any first-in-Principal intervention, (b) any Tier 2+ protocol initiation, (c) any new intervention class, (d) any protocol change after a Grade 2 AE. **This fixes the F4 (CMO single point of failure) flaw at Phase 0, not deferred to Phase 1.**
- **Compensation & Conflicts Committee** (3 independents): C-suite comp, spinout equity allocations, recusal disputes
- **Risk & Security Committee** (LID + CSecO + 1 independent): Risk register, jurisdictional contingency, Principal personal security

### External Ethics Committee (EEC) — NEW

A **truly independent** 5-member body, **paid from irrevocable escrow** the Principal cannot claw back. Membership:
- Senior bioethicist (chair)
- Two non-program physicians from outside the longevity field
- Disability-rights ethicist
- Lay member

**EEC holds BINDING veto** (not advisory) over an enumerated list:
- Any intervention with primary irreversible mechanism
- Heterologous mitochondrial transfer
- Partial reprogramming protocols
- BCI implantation
- Any Phase 3+ implant
- Any new intervention class not contemplated in original Phase 0 SOP
- Any data-transfer-to-spinout instance (case by case)

Dissolving or restructuring the EEC **automatically triggers a hard stop** and 90-day cooling-off. The Principal's recourse against an EEC veto is to absorb the cost of the Wind-Down Reserve forfeiture (see §1.4 Override Mechanism).

### Override Mechanism (Real Friction)

The Principal can override a Board+SAB+EEC "Fail" determination at any gate, **but only**:

1. After mandatory **90-day cooling-off**
2. With written acknowledgment of each specific objection, item by item
3. After commissioning + personally reviewing a second-opinion from the standing External Evaluation Panel
4. With **automatic forfeiture of the $50M Wind-Down Reserve** to the institutional entity (not the Principal's estate)

The Principal further pre-commits, in the **Ulysses Contract** (doc 12 §12.11), that:
- 3-of-11 KPI outcome at G4 triggers automatic Phase-1 budget reduction to maintenance-only, reversible only after 6-month cooling-off + external review.
- If cognitive composite declines >1.5 SD from baseline, unilateral override authority is suspended pending Board+EEP+proxy review.

## 1.5 Critical Success Factors

1. **The Principal's safety.** No KPI is worth a Grade 4 AE. The CMO has veto power. The Science & Safety Committee has binding pre-clearance. The EEC has binding veto on enumerated list.
2. **Legal sustainability.** No intervention in a jurisdiction where it would be criminal. The GC has veto power. Co-primary jurisdictions (Próspera + UAE/DHCC + El Salvador) prevent single-point regulatory failure.
3. **Scientific integrity.** All protocols pre-registered on public registry; all data preserved; EEP review at each gate; **public pre-registration** of KPI thresholds at M0 prevents drift.
4. **IP capture.** Every meaningful discovery filed within 30 days; every spinout structured before Series A.
5. **Reputational management.** Press strategy by exception; no media engagement without GC approval; the Principal does not discuss the program publicly until Phase 2 at earliest.

## 1.6 Constraints

- **Capital:** $62M Phase 0 hard cap, pre-funded into irrevocable escrow at T0; subsequent phases gated.
- **Time:** Phase 0 must complete in 18 months; slip beyond 24 = Board review.
- **Geography:** Primary R&D in Próspera ZEDE; co-primary in UAE/DHCC and El Salvador; satellite labs only in jurisdictions on GC-approved list. **No interventional procedures in US, EU, UK.**
- **Personnel:** No more than 30% of staff from any single nationality (counterintelligence consideration).
- **Public disclosure:** Phase 0 KPI thresholds and protocol skeleton pre-registered publicly at M0 (integration thesis redacted). Other disclosure by exception until Phase 2 gate.

## 1.7 Assumptions

1. The Principal remains medically eligible (no terminal diagnosis, no incapacitating injury) through Phase 0.
2. At least one of (Próspera ZEDE, UAE/DHCC, El Salvador) survives current legal challenges through at least 2030. (Co-primary structure means we are no longer single-jurisdiction dependent.)
3. No global moratorium on somatic gene therapy or partial reprogramming is enacted.
4. Capital markets do not collapse to the point where Phase 1 capital ($1B+) becomes unavailable.
5. No competing program produces a definitive negative result that invalidates the integration thesis.
