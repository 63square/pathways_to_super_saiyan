# 11 — Pilot Subject Protocol (Standard Operating Procedure) — v1.1

## SOP — Conduct of Phase 0 Interventions on the Principal

**Document classification:** PROMETHEUS-SECRET — for CMO, treating physicians, Principal, GC, EEC chair only.
**Effective:** Upon CMO countersignature + EEC binding approval + Próspera Ethics Council approval.
**Review cadence:** Bi-monthly during Phase 0.

**v1.1 revisions (post-SAB):** (a) Stop rules recalibrated for enhancement context (Grade 2 = hard stop; persistent Grade 1 >30d = soft stop); (b) Psychiatric stop rules added (suicidal ideation, hypomania, PHQ-9/GAD-7 thresholds); (c) Relational stop rules added; (d) Quarterly capacity + therapeutic-misconception assessments by external psychiatrist + bioethicist; (e) "Never List" expanded beyond germline; (f) Two-physician refusal rule; (g) Drug substitutions per doc 02 v1.1; (h) Sperm cryo + sexual function + structured psych battery added to baseline; (i) Cumulative toxicity ceiling.

## 11.1 Subject Definition

The Principal is:
- An adult, mentally competent individual
- The funder of and primary subject of Project PROMETHEUS
- Recipient of all interventions described herein
- The sole party with veto authority over any procedure on their own body — subject to:
  - **EEC binding veto** on enumerated list (DEFINITIONS §4)
  - **Two-physician refusal** rule (§11.5)
  - **Ulysses contract** on prior commitments (doc 12)

The Principal has been informed of:
- Investigational nature of all Phase 0 interventions
- Off-label nature of pharmacological combinations
- Right to withdraw at any time without penalty
- Possibility of AEs including death, although low-probability
- **Operational target (DEFINITIONS §2.2) — not canonical SSJ**
- **Realistic envelope (doc 02 §2.3) of 3–5× elite baseline on best axes, not naive 12–80×**

## 11.3 Consent Process (Strengthened)

### Master Consent (signed per phase)
- Plain-language description; all anticipated interventions listed with citations
- Right to withdraw at any time
- Independent counsel signature certifying explanation
- **DEFINITIONS.md acknowledgment** (Principal confirms operational target understanding)

### Procedure-Specific Consent (per intervention)
- Specific intervention name, dose, route
- Citations to evidence base
- Specific risks with quantified probabilities
- Specific alternatives, including "do nothing"
- **14-day cooling-off** for any first-time intervention
- **24-hour cooling-off** for repeat interventions

### Data Use Consent (annual)
- Authorizes use of de-identified data for spinout commercial purposes **with EEC binding pre-approval for each transfer instance**
- Authorizes use of identified data only for medical care + Board-approved purposes
- **Explicit prohibition** on intervention whose primary justification is data generation rather than clinical benefit (EEC determines)

### Advance Directive (quarterly updated, multi-jurisdictional)
- Parallel directives executed under each operating jurisdiction's formalities (Próspera, UAE, El Salvador, Cayman) — defeats recognition gaps
- Care preferences in incapacity
- **Ulysses clause** for specific pre-commitments (per doc 12)
- Designated medical proxy + alternate + tertiary (per doc 06 §6.8)
- Cryopreservation / posthumous data use directives

### NEW: Capacity Acknowledgment
- Quarterly baseline MacCAT-CR (capacity to consent to research)
- Pre-each-new-intervention-class MacCAT-CR
- Capacity threshold: if any single assessment falls below, **all elective interventions pause** until two independent re-evaluations clear
- Principal cannot fire the assessors

### NEW: Therapeutic Misconception Screen
- Quarterly, external psychiatrist + bioethicist dyad
- Pre-registered questions: "Do you believe these interventions will extend your life? On what evidence?" "What outcome would cause you to stop?"
- Documented in personnel file

## 11.4 Intervention Approval Workflow

For **any** intervention performed on the Principal:

```
1. PI proposes intervention to CSO + CMO (written protocol)
2. CMO + CSO review for safety and scientific merit
3. Statistical Analysis Plan locked by Head of Biostat (independent of CSO bias)
4. If new intervention class: EEC binding review + Próspera Ethics Council review (≤30 days)
5. Science & Safety Committee BINDING pre-clearance (Board sub-committee)
6. If approved: Procedure-Specific Consent drafted by GC + CMO
7. Principal reviews with independent counsel (separate firm from GC)
8. Cooling-off period (14 days first-time; 24h repeat)
9. Day-of-procedure: pre-procedure medical clearance
10. Procedure performed by named treating team only
11. Post-procedure monitoring (minimum 24 hours observed)
12. Data captured to EMR + program database + Tier-Red enclave
13. AE monitoring per CTCAE v5 with recalibrated enhancement-context thresholds
14. Follow-up labs + clinical at 24h, 72h, 1 wk, 4 wk minimum
```

No step skipped or compressed without written CMO + GC + S&S Committee + Principal sign-off.

## 11.5 Stop Rules — RECALIBRATED FOR ENHANCEMENT CONTEXT

**v1.1 critical change per SAB:** v1.0 used oncology-trial thresholds (Grade 3 = stop) which are calibrated to dying patients. Enhancement context requires healthy-volunteer thresholds.

### Hard Stops (irreversible halt pending full EEC + S&S + SAB review)

- **Death or imminent life threat** (any cause)
- **Grade 4 AE** (CTCAE v5: life-threatening)
- **Grade 3 AE** (CTCAE v5: severe but not immediately life-threatening) — was soft stop
- **NEW: Grade 2 AE attributable to program intervention** — was soft stop, now hard stop (per SAB)
- **New diagnosis of malignancy** (any stage, any tissue)
- **Cardiac arrest, MI, stroke, significant arrhythmia**
- **Liver failure** (ALT/AST >10× ULN OR INR >2 OR clinical jaundice + bilirubin >3)
- **Acute kidney injury** (creatinine >2× baseline OR oliguria <0.5 mL/kg/hr × 6h)
- **Anaphylaxis or severe allergic reaction**
- **NEW psychiatric hard stops (per SAB):**
  - New suicidal ideation
  - Hypomania
  - Psychotic symptoms
  - Persistent anhedonia
  - PHQ-9 ≥15 OR GAD-7 ≥15 sustained 2 weeks
  - New substance-use escalation
  → immediate pause of all neuro-active and hormonal interventions
- **Principal request to stop** (for any reason; no override)

### Soft Stops (pause + 7-day S&S Committee review)

- **NEW: Persistent Grade 1 AE >30 days** (per SAB — chronic low-grade toxicity is the long-horizon risk)
- **Hematocrit >55%** sustained
- **Liver enzymes 3–10× ULN**
- **Creatinine 1.5–2× baseline**
- **New cardiac structural change on imaging**
- **New persistent symptom** (headache >1 week, fatigue >2 weeks, mood change)
- **Principal's subjective severe distress** (no override)
- **CMO clinical concern** (no override)

### NEW: Cumulative Toxicity Rule (per SAB)

**>3 soft stops in any 12-month window** = mandatory 6-month program-wide pause + external EEC + SAB review.

### NEW: Relational Stop Rules (per SAB)

- Documented social withdrawal
- Divorce / estrangement attributable to program demands
- Loss of pre-program meaningful relationships
→ EEC review of net-harm

### Procedural Stops (re-confirm before continuing)

- Any unplanned hospitalization
- Any new prescription medication
- Any new diagnosis (non-cancer, non-organ-failure)
- Significant life event affecting adherence capacity

### NEW: Two-Physician Refusal Rule (per SAB)

**If any two members of the treating team in good faith decline to perform a requested intervention on safety/ethical grounds, that intervention cannot be performed by substitution of other personnel within the program.** Anti-doctor-shopping. Any clinician termination during active intervention cycle requires EEC review + 60-day stay.

### Restart Criteria

After hard stop: full external EEC + SAB review + Ethics Council re-approval + Principal re-consent + 30-day minimum cooling-off.

After soft stop: S&S Committee + CMO + CSO review + Principal re-consent + 7-day cooling-off.

After cumulative-toxicity 6-month pause: full EEC + SAB + external bioethics review.

## 11.7 Data Capture

Every clinical interaction generates:
- EMR entry (timestamped, locked, audit-trailed)
- Program database entry linked to EMR
- Lab data via HL7 / FHIR through inspected proxy (Tier-Amber → Tier-Red one-way data diode)
- Wearable data via vendor APIs (encrypted at rest)

Data QC: PMO audits randomly selected records monthly. Head of Biostat reviews data integrity quarterly.

Principal data **never** transmitted across public internet in unencrypted form. Physical media transport (WORM) for major Tier-Red datasets.

## 11.8 Adverse Event Reporting

Per CTCAE v5 with recalibrated enhancement-context thresholds.

- **Grade 1 (mild):** documented; weekly treating team huddle
- **Grade 1 persistent >30 days:** soft stop
- **Grade 2 (moderate):** **HARD stop**; CMO + CSO + GC notified within 1 hour; Board + EEC within 24 hours
- **Grade 3+ (severe / life-threatening):** Hard stop; CMO + CEO + Principal + Board + EEC + EEP within 1 hour; crisis comms activated; external regulatory counsel notified; **mandatory dose-escalation committee** convenes
- **Grade 5 (death):** Full crisis protocol; independent autopsy by pre-contracted academic pathology service

All Grade 1+ events trigger AE Report to Próspera Ethics Council within 7 days. Cumulative reports quarterly.

## 11.9 Drug-Specific SOPs (Selected — full library in QMS)

### Senolytic — Dasatinib + Quercetin (SINGLE CYCLE Phase 0, per SAB)

**Indication:** Senolytic single-cycle per Kirkland protocol — NOT monthly
**Dose:** Dasatinib 100mg + Quercetin 1000mg PO × 3 consecutive days
**Frequency:** **Single cycle in Phase 0.** 90-day washout. Pleural ultrasound at 14 days. Monthly cycling considered only if (a) zero AE Grade 1+, (b) no QTc change, (c) no pleural effusion on US.
**Pre:** CBC, BMP, liver panel within 7 days. Cardiac assessment (ECG; echo if concern). Hold if QTc >450 ms, platelets <100K, ANC <1500.
**During:** Vital signs Q4h; symptom monitoring.
**Post:** CBC + BMP + liver at 48h, 7d. Senescence biomarkers at 14d (β-gal, p16, SASP cytokines). **Pleural ultrasound at 14d (per SAB).**
**AE surveillance:** Thrombocytopenia, pleural effusion (any: hard stop dasatinib per recalibrated rules), QTc prolongation (>500 ms: hold).

### Therapeutic Plasma Exchange (CAPPED 4 sessions Phase 0)

**Indication:** Plasma factor dilution
**Procedure:** 1.0 plasma volume exchange with 5% albumin replacement
**Frequency:** **4 sessions total in Phase 0** (was weekly × 4 + Q6M maintenance; capped per SAB feedback to detect chronic immune cost)
**Pre:** CBC, BMP, calcium, magnesium within 24h; IgG level; **B-cell repertoire sequencing baseline + post each session (per SAB — detect immune attrition)**; central line if not in place
**During:** Continuous cardiac monitoring; citrate symptom monitoring (replace Ca PRN); 1:1 nursing
**Post:** 4h observation; same-day discharge from clinical suite. **Vaccine response challenge at M6 (zoster + Tdap baseline + post-TPE) to detect immune cost (per SAB).**
**AE surveillance:** Citrate toxicity, allergic reaction, hypotension.

### Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (DE-ESCALATED per SAB)

**Indication:** Cognitive + recovery enhancement
**Procedure:** **90 min @ 1.5 ATA** (de-escalated from 2.0 ATA; Hachmo protocol not independently replicated), 100% O₂ via mask
**Frequency:** **3×/week × 8 weeks** (de-escalated from 5×/wk per SAB; pulmonary O₂ toxicity accumulates non-linearly)
**Pre:** PFT (rule out trapped gas); ENT (Eustachian); ophth (refractive awareness)
**Monitoring:** **Pulmonary function panels at weeks 0/4/8** (NEW per SAB)
**During:** Vital signs continuous via in-chamber; intercom comms
**AE surveillance:** Otic barotrauma, refractive change (transient), pulmonary O₂ toxicity (Lorrain-Smith), seizure (rare at 1.5 ATA)

### Weekly Low-Dose Rapamycin (NEW per SAB — Tier 1)

**Indication:** PEARL protocol; immune rejuvenation
**Dose:** Per Konopka/Mannick PEARL trial dosing — typically 5mg weekly or equivalent
**Frequency:** Weekly, gated on Head of QA SOP and monthly bloods
**Pre:** Lipid panel, glucose, immune function baseline (CD4/CD8 ratio)
**Monitoring:** Monthly bloods; quarterly lipid + glucose; quarterly **vaccine response challenge** (K9)
**AE surveillance:** Hyperlipidemia, hyperglycemia, mucositis, immunosuppression markers

### Microdose Tirzepatide (NEW per SAB — Tier 1)

**Indication:** Body composition optimization (ApoB, hsCRP, visceral fat); muscle-sparing protocol with resistance training paired
**Dose:** Sub-anti-obesity (typically 25–50% of standard starting dose), titrated
**Frequency:** Weekly sub-Q
**Pre:** Lipase, gastric emptying assessment, current metabolic panel
**Monitoring:** Weekly lipase first month then monthly; FFM quarterly (K4 ensure protein/training paired); CGM (K10)
**AE surveillance:** Pancreatitis (lipase 3× ULN: hard stop), gastric paresis, FFM loss

## 11.13 NEW — "Never List" Beyond Germline (per SAB)

Categorically prohibited interventions (EEC + Board + Principal cannot collectively approve these — bright-line):

1. **Germline modification of any kind**
2. **Heterologous mitochondrial transfer** until peer-reviewed Phase 2 human data exists
3. **Any intervention whose primary mechanism is irreversible without explant or rescue pathway**
4. **Permanent organ ablation**
5. **Irreversible neural lesioning**
6. **Non-removable BCI with no explant pathway**
7. **Pediatric or third-party donor tissue** sourced from jurisdictions without Declaration of Istanbul-equivalent donor consent
8. **Any intervention performed in international waters specifically because unlawful on land**
9. **Any intervention whose expected toxicity exceeds CTCAE Grade 3** for enhancement (non-therapeutic) indication
10. **Any data-generation intervention** without clinical benefit to Principal (EEC determines)

The Never List can be expanded by Principal signature; **narrowed only with unanimous Board + EEC binding approval**.

## 11.14 NEW — Dignity-of-Risk Ceiling (per SAB)

A. **Maximum cumulative risk budget** per phase, expressed in expected QALY loss or equivalent. When cumulative procedural risk crosses pre-specified threshold, all elective escalation halts pending external review regardless of Principal consent.

B. **Two-physician refusal** rule (§11.5 above).

C. **Anti-doctor-shopping**: terminate-and-replace-with-compliant prevented by 60-day EEC stay.

D. **Program dissolution clause**: Principal signs explicit commitment that program reserves right to dissolve itself if leadership concludes in good faith that continuation would be net-harmful. Such dissolution is not breach. Liquidated damages waiving Principal's right to sue for safety-grounded closure.
